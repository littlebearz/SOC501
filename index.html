Key Terms:

  • Commodified: processes through which good/services, including labour, become commodities bought and sold in the market
  • Dirty Work: work that is physically disgusting, a symbol of degradation
  • Emotional Labour: work requiring suppression of personal emotions to please and manipulate the customers’ demeanor and emotion
  • Standardization: process of making practices more uniform and standard
  • Urbanization: increase population living in urban settings

Services for Sale

Factors that contributed to Service Industry Growth

  • - Commodification of Goods & Services
  • - Urbanization
  • - Promotion of Fast Food Industry
  • - Segregated Work

Commodification of Goods & Services

  • Services and goods are purchased rather than made in the past
  • Historically, families were self-reliant and only used community barter & exchange for things they could not manufacture on their own

Urbanization

  • Lead to more demand for purchased goods, services which include policing and other forms of protective work
  • Improved roads and railways allowed easier travel which increases the likelihood people would need to acquire things away from home

"Work and Spend Cycle"

  • People work longer hours but are compensated with a higher standard of living
  • Feel compelled to spend more on goods & services but have to spend more time at work in order to keep up with spending habit

Promotion of Fast Food Industry

  • Marketing techniques used to portray eating out as affordable alternative from eating at home

Segregated Work

  • Work is segregated and shaped by gender, race, ethnicity and age
  • Industry Growth appears to be co-related with increased immigration

Serving the Public

  • Service work is intangible, this constitutes lower wages because it is difficult to assign monetary value to something you can't touch
  • Workers are required to bring identity and self-expression to work

Emotional Labour

  • Workers can be fired for not having a good demeanor
  • This causes worker strain because the lack of autonomy causes alienation from their work
  • However, service jobs can also be satisfying

Types of Service Work

Type of Service Work Description
Restaurant
  • - Less education
  • - Expected to work on short notice
  • - Low pay
  • - Repetitive and standardized work and requires emotional labour
  • - Limited autonomy and close supervision
Type of Service Work Description
Domestic and Cleaning Service Work
  • - Low status dirty work
  • - Performed mostly by immigrant female workers
  • - Isolates and subject to exploitation and abuse
  • - Cleaners have little autonomy
Type of Service Work Description
Protective Services
  • - Police officers, firefighters
  • - More male dominated
  • - More autonomy
  • - Emotional labour - have to suppress fear
Type of Service Work Description
Other Services
  • - Early Childhood Educators, Child Care Workers
  • - Earn less but have similar educational requirements as police officers
  • - Female dominated
  • - More autonomy
